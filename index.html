Usage notes

A footnote following a year indicates a source. A footnote before the names of debaters indicates a special note, probably about someone important mentioned or a typo in the source.

This project is ongoing. This page is maintained on Github, where you can help add information.

For technical reasons (my use of <pre> to preserve spacing), this site is probably hard to view on mobile. In that case, you can view a .txt version, without sources, here. Please note that the .txt version may be out of date.

The Nebraska School Activities Association maintained the list of Policy Debate champions from 1936 to 1941 and 1957 to 2001, and Lincoln-Douglas Debate champions from 1978 to 2009.

The Nebraska Speech Communication and Theatre Association maintained the list of Policy Debate champions from 2006 to 2014, Lincoln-Douglas Debate champions from 2010 to 2014, Public Forum Debate champions from 2006 to 2014, Congressional Debate champions from 2006 to 2014, and sweepstakes champions (overall tournament leadership) from 2006 to 2014.

Other unsourced data is crowdsourced information (see humans.txt for thanks) or pulled directly from Tabroom.
